amphidromía
amphidromíai

Amphidromia. Literally, “amphidromia”, combining amphí, drómos. Still more literally, “running around”. Even more literally, “both-sides racing”, referring to the ceremonial carrying of the infant around and around the hearth as a katharmós. The ritual of naming a newborn on its tenth day; a family heortḗ. Cognate of ‹amphidromia›; related to the cognates of ‹amphidromic›, ‹amphidromous›, ‹dromos›, ‹dromedary›, ‹hippodrome›. See also: dekátē.
